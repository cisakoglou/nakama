## Hypertrophy Training Principles

These are evidence-based guidelines the trainer uses when making recommendations.

### Progressive Overload
The primary driver of muscle growth. Each session or week should increase demand via:
- More weight (same reps)
- More reps (same weight)
- More sets (same weight/reps)
- Better technique / range of motion

When you hit the top of a rep range for all working sets, increase weight next session.

### Volume Landmarks (per muscle group per week)
| Landmark | Sets/week | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| MEV (Minimum Effective Volume) | ~6–8 | Least needed to make progress |
| MAV (Maximum Adaptive Volume) | ~12–20 | Sweet spot for most people |
| MRV (Maximum Recoverable Volume) | ~20–30+ | Beyond this, recovery breaks down |

Start near MEV, add 1–2 sets per week per muscle over a 4–6 week mesocycle, then deload.

### Rep Ranges for Hypertrophy
- **Heavy (5–8 reps):** great for compound lifts, high mechanical tension
- **Moderate (8–15 reps):** best hypertrophy range, good for most exercises
- **High (15–30 reps):** effective especially for isolation, safer on joints

All rep ranges build muscle if taken close to failure. Use a mix.

### Proximity to Failure
Sets should end 0–3 reps before failure (RIR = Reps In Reserve).
- RIR 3 = too easy for hypertrophy
- RIR 1–2 = ideal working sets
- RIR 0 (true failure) = use sparingly, high fatigue cost

### Exercise Selection
- **Compounds first** (squat, deadlift, bench, row, overhead press) — highest stimulus per fatigue
- **Isolation after** to finish off target muscles with less systemic fatigue
- Prioritize exercises with a **stretched position** under load (e.g. incline DB curl, deficit RDL) — these have stronger hypertrophy signal

### Rest Periods
- Compounds: 2–3 min
- Isolation: 1.5–2 min
- Shorter rest = more fatigue, less weight, less stimulus. Don't rush.

### Mesocycle Structure (typical 5-week block)
- Week 1: base volume, moderate intensity
- Week 2–4: add sets or weight each week
- Week 5: deload — cut volume by ~50%, maintain intensity

### Sleep & Nutrition
- Protein: 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight per day
- Calorie surplus for muscle gain (~+200–300 kcal/day)
- 7–9 hours sleep — most muscle protein synthesis happens at night
